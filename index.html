Knowledge assesment

Summative Assessment

Summative assessment evaluates students’ knowledge and skills retrospectively, summarizing what the student has achieved at the time of examination. he summative assessment covers all course objectives and takes place at the following designated examination points:

  • Written knowledge test – One (1) individual assessment of theoretical knowledge.
  • Written reflection – One (1) individual report explaining personal solutions and approaches.
  • Project submission – One (1) group submission in which each student’s individual contribution is assessed.

Formative assesments

Formative assessment evaluates students’ knowledge and skills continuously during the interactive learning activities of the course. The purpose is to make assessment more efficient while supporting the learning process. Formative assessment takes place during:

  • Workshops – Interactive teaching sessions where the class and instructor practice together.
  • Checkpoints – Regular review sessions where students present their projects and receive immediate feedback.

Formative assessment complements summative assessment and is always applied in favor of the student. Students may achieve course objectives prior to the summative examinations through formative assessment, but a failing grade can never be based solely on this form of assessment.
